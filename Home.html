About Me

I am a Postgraduate student at Cape Peninsula University of Technology, pursuing a degree in Mathematical Sciences. My academic journey has been marked by a strong passion for mathematics and its applications in various fields. I have developed a solid foundation in mathematical concepts and techniques, which has enabled me to excel in my studies and engage in research projects. I am particularly interested in areas such as data analysis, data science, machine learning, and mathematical modeling. Through my coursework and extracurricular activities, I have honed my problem-solving skills and gained practical experience in applying mathematical principles to real-world challenges. I am eager to continue expanding my knowledge and skills in mathematics, with the goal of making meaningful contributions to the field and pursuing a career that allows me to utilize my expertise.

Why I am pursuing a career in Mathematical Sciences?

Growing up, it honestly felt like punishment sometimes. While other kids were out playing soccer and enjoying their childhood, I was in the workshop holding a work lamp. But that is where it all started. My childhood was full of fixing things, figuring out problems, and helping people get their vehicles back on the road, it is what my family did. Somewhere along the way, I did not just learn it… I started to love it.
High school changed the setting, but not who I was. I spent most of my time in class, away from the workshop, but the problem-solving never stopped. With limited options for sport, I leaned into academics. That is when I found Mathematics. It had more problems than any other subject and that is exactly what pulled me in. I got hooked. What started as just another subject turned into something I genuinely enjoyed, and it opened doors I never even thought about before.
Now, I am a data analyst but not just because it is my job. I do it because I love solving problems and making sense of things that do not immediately make sense. I enjoy digging into the details, finding patterns, and turning them into something useful. Some things do not speak out loud but if you pay attention, they tell you everything you need to know.
